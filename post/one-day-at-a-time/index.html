One Day at a Time

  • Wed, Sep 4, 2024
  • 2-minute read

School had been very rough for me the past few years. From 2021 to 2022, I was trying to figure out what to do next after Bergen Community College. From fall 2022 to fall 2024, Computer Science at Oregon State University wasn’t working out for me. I started my first term at University of Maryland Global Campus in August, and I tried extra hard because I wanted to succeed. I threw away all my hobbies, I deleted some social media accounts, and I deleted my personal data.

Of course, the need for proper mental stimulation soon arose. After two weeks of trying to focus solely on a trade and a professional life, I couldn’t handle that anymore. There’s something to be said about “the definition of insanity,” though. I’m not going back to Twitter/X, Mastodon, or Bluesky. It’s the free for all nature of those platforms where anyone can talk about whatever when I need things to be focused on my hobbies and interests. Also, I’ve never understood the act of “retweeting.” While I require Discord for school purposes, I’m not going to chase down all the Discord servers that I was a part of. I just don’t understand Discord.

Anyway, I’m now picking up the pieces of what I did about two weeks ago. I have three hard drives. All my data alphabetically after the Myrient directory in the root of the third hard drive is lost. That includes my “Retro Computing and Windows 9x” directory. I know that I could run a scan on the drive using data recovery tools, but there would be a million files that I wouldn’t recognize if I did that. The best that I can do there is use one of those data recovery tools for inspiration on what to look for online.

All my files related to the Yuzu emulator might be tough to find, and I know that my Gravis Ultrasound preconfigured directory for emulators that I found on VOGONS is offline. Thankfully, someone else on VOGONS created a new directory that is mirrored on the Internet Archive. People tell me this all the time, but it’s true that I have to take things one day at a time.
